# qtpc
Open-Source Quantum Topologically Protected Computer 

With the breakthrough found in this paper [Revealing the topological nature of entangled orbital angular momentum states of light](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-66066-3) , we now only need to alter the phase of the wavefront to create 48 distinct channels. Because the channels are actual twists of the light ( altering the phase creates whole numbered integer twists of orbital angular momentum which physically will never 'bump into each other' ) , they are very stable; we can alter their amplitude (brightness) and easily distinguish channel 32 has value .4 and channel 33 has value 1 without any bleed through of data - this is the topological protection part.

The Quantum part we achieve by creating entagled photon pairs , and now instead of two 48 channels for 96 values total, we hijack the entire probability state space for the 48x48 channels or 2304 values. By altering one of Zone A's 48 states we immediately and instantly alter all of Zone B's 48 states since the photons are entagled, this is where our extra space comes from. This is the physical mechanism of quantum computing.

And finally after writing each of photon A and B's wavefronts, we bring them back together in a beam-splitter where the mid air collision creates waves of interference and destruction, either solving our carefully crafted 'shortest path' problem, or giving us the results of lights natural ability to perform **matrix cross-product multiplication**
