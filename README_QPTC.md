# Quantum Topologically Protected Computer

There was a huge breakthrough recently in [this paper](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-025-66066-3.pdf). 
They figured out that you only need OAM to create an optical topology. 
For 20 years everyone thought you would need at least two pieces of info from the light, like polarization and OAM.

Instead of encoding data into our probability matrix, we would turn the entire state space into a super stable topological knot. The paper illustrates 17,000 unique invariants , so we would have an alphabet of 17,000.

And here we have to get creative. We would have to start assigning meaning to Knot#127 , and we could transform it with Knot#150 to get a completely new and unique shape ( Knot#127x150 ).
